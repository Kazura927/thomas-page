end of the year thoughts

just me rambling about december.

I've never really liked Christmas.

It's not that I want to be edgy or contrarian — it's just that this time of year always makes me feel a little too sensitive.
Kind of like birthdays, I guess.

Every year, even if I don't say it out loud, I secretly hope something really good will happen. Not anything specific, just something that feels warm. Unexpected. Special.
And it's not like nothing happens... it's just that I usually end up feeling a little disappointed.
Not because of anyone in particular — just because I went into it hoping for too much. It's that kind of quiet, nostalgic expectation that almost never gets fulfilled.

Sometimes I even feel a bit lonely.
Everything around me seems to remind me that another year has gone by — quietly, quickly, and without warning.

And yeah... it's kind of scary.
How fast things change.
How people drift apart.
How different everything feels each December.

But somehow, I still like New Year's.

It feels cleaner.
Quieter.
Not because of the fireworks or the countdown — I don't really care about any of that — but because of the idea of starting over.
Even if I don't believe in the whole “new year, new me” thing, there's something comforting in thinking:
“I made it this far. I can do it again.”

I've been thinking about this — trying to reflect on why I always feel this way.
And maybe it's because, deep down, I'm still hoping that someone else will do something that makes it all feel meaningful.
But I guess I need to learn to appreciate the small things a bit more.
And maybe start living up to my own expectations, instead of waiting for something magical to happen.

I know that probably won't stop me from feeling this same way next December 24th...
but for now, I just wanted to write it down.
Just in case I forget how I felt tonight.

Thanks for stopping.
